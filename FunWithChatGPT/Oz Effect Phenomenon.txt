Q: Tell me about the Oz effect 

The Oz Effect refers to a phenomenon where individuals experience a heightened sense of 
awareness, perception, or even mystical sensations while in natural or remote environments, 
often accompanied by feelings of awe and transcendence. The term was popularized by 
researcher Dr. Robert K. French in the 1970s, drawing inspiration from the fictional land of Oz 
in L. Frank Baum's "Wizard of Oz" series. It's often associated with experiences in wilderness 
or remote areas, where the vastness and beauty of nature can trigger intense emotional responses. 
However, it's worth noting that the Oz Effect is not universally recognized within scientific circles 
and remains a subject of ongoing debate.
